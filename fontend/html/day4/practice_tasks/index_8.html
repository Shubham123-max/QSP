Article 2: How To Create a Resilient Personal Financial PlanBy shub

Setting up a financial plan with a clear, specific purpose is called goal-based investing. Instead of chasing short-term returns, you build a pool of funds within a defined timeframe to achieve your life objectives. Experts recommend setting aside time to identify your needs and categorizing them into three stages:Short-term objectives (1–3 years): This involves clearing credit card debt, building an emergency fund, or planning a holiday.Medium-term goals (3–7 years): This usually includes saving for a car, accumulating a home down payment, or funding an upcoming wedding.Long-term goals (7+ years): This focuses on wealth creation, your children's education, and retirement planning.
